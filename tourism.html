Santos: History & Sea

Santos seamlessly blends a rich historical legacy with a dynamic coastal lifestyle.

  • The Monte Serrat Funicular Elevator is a historic railway connecting downtown Santos with Monte Serrat.
  • At the summit, a lookout point offers panoramic views of the city and port.
  • The Pelé Museum houses a collection of memorabilia, trophies, and photographs documenting the famous football player's life and career.
  • The Santos Tourist Tram Line provides a nostalgic ride passing by significant sights in the historic city center.
  • The Fish Market (Mercado de Peixes), opened in its new home in 2020, is a traditional spot to explore the daily coastal catch.
  • The Pinacoteca Benedicto Calixto exhibits seventy works by the important Brazilian painter in a stunning white mansion right on the beach.

📸 Local Tip: For dining, skip the basics and seek out bistros that craft their dishes with robust regional ingredients, such as utilizing local cachaça to glaze seafood and pairing it with rich mushroom reductions.

Bertioga: Pristine Beaches & Heritage: where the sea mountain meets the ocean

🏰 Historical Landmarks

Forte São João, built in 1532, is one of the oldest fortresses in Brazil.

It offers guided tours, preserved military architecture, and stunning views over the Bertioga Canal.

🌊 Iconic Beaches

  • Praia da Enseada is a 12 km long beach known for its calm waters and family-friendly environment.
  • Riviera de São Lourenço is an affluent, master-planned neighborhood with excellent tourist infrastructure surrounded by palm trees.
  • Itaguaré Beach is a semidesert beach completely surrounded by preserved rainforest, making it a spectacular location for photography and surfing.

If you're seeking breathtaking natural scenery, the juxtaposition of the Atlantic Forest mountains plunging into clear coastal waters here is truly spectacular.

Parque Estadual da Serra do Mar

For hikers, trail runners, and nature enthusiasts, the surrounding mountains are a sanctuary.

  • The park features incredibly diverse ecosystems and protects the largest continuous remnant of the Atlantic Forest in Brazil.
  • The Caminhos do Mar historical park contains the preserved Pontilhão Da Serra and historical monuments celebrating the independence of Brazil.
  • The region operates as a recognized UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, preserving countless endemic species of flora and fauna.

These trails offer a profound escape, allowing you to walk historically significant paths while completely enveloped by untouched nature.

🥾 Ecotourism Tips

Tackling the trails or enjoying a canoe trip down the mountain rivers requires an early start. Bring proper gear, and don't forget to pack a thermos of coffee or tea to enjoy when you finally reach a clearing overlooking the vast coastline below.
